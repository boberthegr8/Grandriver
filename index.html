Where you'll ride

The stretch below Dunnville runs wide and calm — good water for anyone new to a PWC. Follow it south and you're through the Port Maitland piers into open Erie. Locals run the shoreline east toward Rock Point on a flat morning.

About Brad

Owner, mechanic, and the guy handing you the key

Brad grew up a few hundred metres from where the Grand meets Erie. His family kept a boat at Port Maitland before he could walk, and he's been on this water every summer since — first on his dad's ski boat, then on whatever ski he could keep running through a season.

He's spent his working life around personal watercraft: wrenching on them, running them, and pulling people off them when a weekend rider gets in over their head. Grand River Rentals came out of that — a small fleet he maintains himself, so he actually knows what condition every machine is in when it leaves the dock.

"If it isn't right, it doesn't go out. That's the whole business model."

You'll get a real briefing, not a waiver and a shrug. If you've never been on a ski, say so — Brad will walk you through the throttle, the no-wake zones, and where the sandbars sit. If you've been riding for twenty years, he'll point you east toward Rock Point and get out of your way.
